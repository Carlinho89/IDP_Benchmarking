CCR Model

 

The Traditional model

The work of Charnes, Cooper and Rhodes (1978) has marked the starting point to all DEA-related studies. From an economical point of view, the transformation from inputs (e.g. shots on goal, wages paid) into outputs (e.g. goals scored, titles won) is a production process that may be described by a production frontier. DMUs on this frontier are considered as efficient, the ones below are inefficient in comparison to these. The main objective of CCR-DEA is to create an overall efficiency score that is measuring a DMU’s relative efficiency. Sample Size

To ensure a valid differentiation between efficient and inefficient units, CCR-DEA needs a relatively big sample size. Two different rules of thumb for an ideal sample size are advised depending on the source: Dyson et al (2001): Number of DMUs ≥ 2 * (Number of Inputs * Number of Outputs) Cooper et al (2007): Number of DMUs ≥ 3 * (Number of Inputs + Number of Outputs)


Input- or Output-Orientation

The above model is an input-oriented CCR-model, in which emphasis is put on the reduction of input. To decide whether to use an input- or output-oriented model, it is necessary to consider which part of the production process can be influenced by the DMU. If a DMU is in control over its inputs, an input-orientated model should be used. The measure of input efficiency reflects the extent to which input levels of a DMU can be lowered through improved performance without lowering any output level. An output-oriented model that focuses on output enlargement is favourable in case of controllable outputs.
